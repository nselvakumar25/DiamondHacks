Your Government

The U.S. divides your government into three branches to ensure power distribution. The three branches include: Legislative, Executive, and Judicial.

Legislative Branch

The Legislative Branch is made up of two houses in Congress: Senate and House of Representatives.

  • Senate: Represents citizens on an equal state basis, thus 2 representatives each state
  • House of Representatives: Based on district populations

    • Key Roles:

  • Proposing and passing laws
  • Confirming or denying nominations for federal agencies, judges, and supreme court
  • Can declare war

    • Executive Branch

    The Executive Branch is made up of the President, Vice President, and the Cabinet.

  • President: Makes treaties, vetoes or signs bills, represents foreign affairs, enforces laws, commander in chief in war, nominates representatives in cabinet, supreme court, and other officials
  • Vice President: Can act in place of President, supports and governs
  • The Cabinet: Advisors to the President

    • Key Roles:

  • Enforces and executes laws
  • Manages daily operations of government
  • Carries out functions of Department of Defense, Justice, and Environmental Protection Agency

    • Judicial Branch

    The Judicial Branch primarily consists of the courts and judges.

  • Courts: Headed by the court system, with the highest court typically being the Supreme Court
  • Judges: Appointed by the Executive branch, responsible for presiding over trials and maintaining order

    • Key Roles:

  • Interpreting the laws and ensuring it is fair
  • Resolving any disputes and protecting individual rights
